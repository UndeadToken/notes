Search Authority
A search warrant, subpoena or consent. Exigent circumstances include child endangerment.

Chain of Custody
The integrity of artefacts, the time its collected to the time its presented to the court. If the chain is broken, the evidence could be excluded from the trial.

Imaging/Hashing Function
A forensic clone (or bitstream) of every bit on the media. The process of creating a bitstream is called imaging. Copies are not acceptable in court unless they mathematically, via hashing, replicate the original media.

Validated Tools
Tools must be tested before they are used to verify the accuracy of their results.

Analysis
Locate and interpret artefacts
- linking some activity with a specific user account
- establishing a timeline of events
- determining whether a usb storage device was connected to a machine
- breaking encryption
- identifying relationships/connections between individuals (i.e suspect andf victim)
- identifying websites that have been visited
- identifying whether certain files were opened or downloaded
- identifying what search engine quiries have been entered
- locating contraband
- determining what applications have been installed or uninstalled
- recovering deleted files
- determining whether or not the system has been compromised in some way

Repeatability (Quality Assurance)
The results of a forensic examination (and the process used to reach them) should be able to be duplicated.

Reporting
- executive summaries
- list of evidence items examined
- methods and tools used to perform analysis
- findings
- conclusion
- relevant exhibits

Possible Expert Presentation
Presentation of the findings to a judge or a jury.
