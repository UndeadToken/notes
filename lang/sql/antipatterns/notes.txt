An Antipattern is a technique that is intended to solve a problem but that often leads to other problems typically implemented by a misunderstanding of database theory.

Logical Database Design Antipatterns
Pre-emptive planning of what information needs to be stored in the database and the best way to organize and interconnect your data including scemas and relationships.

Physical Database Design Antipatterns
Efficiently implementing data management including writing schemas and indexes in addition to selecting data types.

Query Antipatterns
Adding, retrieving and mutating data.

Application Development Antipatterns
Blunders in implementations within a given language e.g. PHP.
